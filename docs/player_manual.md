# Synarchy — Player Manual

## What this game is

Synarchy is a settlement game on a procedurally generated world. You
don't control anyone directly: a small band of acolytes steps out of a
portal, each with a body that needs water, food, sleep, and warmth, and
each making its own decisions. You guide them by marking work to be
done — dig here, fell these trees, farm this ground, build this — and
they weave your orders in with keeping themselves alive. There is no
scripted victory; the goal is to keep the colony alive and growing.

## Starting a game

From the title screen ("Ecce Homo"), choose **Create World**.

Give the world a name — everything else can stay at its defaults for a
first game. Press **Generate World**; generation takes a minute or two
at the default size. When it finishes, press **Continue** to enter the
world (or **Regenerate** to roll a different one).

You arrive looking at an empty world with a toolbar in the bottom-left
corner. Your colonists don't exist yet. Open the build tool, pick the
**Acolyte Portal**, and click somewhere sensible — flat ground, near
fresh water. The portal appears at once and your colony walks out of
it: five acolytes, then a technomule, a pack robot hauling your
starting supplies.

## Getting around

- **Pan** with W/A/S/D or the arrow keys.
- **Rotate** a quarter-turn with Q and E.
- **Zoom** with the mouse wheel. Zooming all the way out fades to a map
  of the whole world; zoom back in to return to the ground.
- **Hold Shift and scroll** to step the view up or down one height
  level — for peering into pits or up cliffs. **Home** returns the
  camera to automatic height-following.

Keys can be rebound in Settings.

## Time

The world runs in real time — day and night, a calendar, growing
seasons. **Space** pauses and unpauses. While paused you can still look
around, select things, and mark out work; nothing moves until you
resume. Note that the Escape menu does **not** pause the game — time
keeps running underneath it, so press Space first if you need it
stopped.

## Directing your acolytes

- **Left-click** a unit to select it. **Shift-click** adds to the
  selection; **drag** a box to select a group. Click empty ground to
  deselect.
- **Right-click** with units selected sends them there. Right-clicking
  a unit, building, or item opens a small menu of actions on it.
- **Escape** is the universal cancel: it closes menus and popups,
  cancels an armed tool, and clears the selection.
- **L** toggles the log panel — a record of recent events.

Orders are requests, not puppet strings: a thirsty acolyte will finish
drinking before following your command, and one in danger looks after
itself first.

The toolbar in the bottom-left holds the work tools: **mine**,
**build**, **chop trees**, **till soil**, **plant crops**, and the
default selection tool. (Some toolbar buttons keep alternates behind a
right-click on the button itself.) The marking tools all work the same
way: one click anchors a corner, a second click marks out the
rectangle; right-click or Escape cancels. Marked work goes on the
colony's shared list and any able acolyte will get to it — over time
they gravitate toward the work they've grown skilled at.

Building works from the same toolbar: choose a building, place its
outline, and your acolytes haul materials to it and put it up.
Workshops (the workbench, the furnace, and their kin) take standing
crafting orders once built.

## Keeping the colony alive

Acolytes see to their own needs when the means exist — they drink from
rivers and canteens, eat what they carry or can forage, and sleep when
exhausted. Your job is to make the means exist:

- **Water:** settle near fresh water.
- **Food:** the starting rations will run out. Foraging bridges the
  gap; farming sustains — till soil, then plant crops on the tilled
  ground, and they will be tended and harvested in season.
- **Injury and death:** falls, cold, hunger, and fighting all wound
  acolytes. Death from wounds is rarely instant — a hurt acolyte that
  stays fed, watered, and rested can pull through.

## Saving, loading, quitting

Press **Escape** in the world to open the menu: **Save**, **Settings**,
**Exit to Menu**, **Exit to Desktop**. Saving pauses the game — press
Space to carry on. On the title screen, **Continue** (shown once you
have a save) resumes the most recent one, and **Load Game** (shown once
you have several) picks among them; a loaded game always starts paused,
so press Space to set it running. There is no autosave — save before
you exit.

## Scope note

This manual deliberately covers only what the screen cannot show: the
keys, the mouse verbs, and the core loop. It does not walk through
every toolbar button, panel, or menu — discovering those on-screen is
part of the game, and (for playtesting) part of the test. Please don't
expand it into a full reference: playtesting uses it as the baseline
for what a new player was told versus what they could figure out, so
it must stay a one-page quickstart.
